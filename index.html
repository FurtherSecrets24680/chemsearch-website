What's actually in it

The useful bits

Not a chemistry encyclopedia pretending to be an app. These are the things you actually reach for when you need an answer quickly — or when your lab partner has disappeared and the Wi-Fi is flaky.

Finds what you mean, not just what you typed

  • Common name, IUPAC, CAS number, or PubChem CID — any of them works.
  • Real-time autocomplete with a scrollable dropdown, so you don't have to nail the spelling of "acetylsalicylic" on the first try.
  • Dedicated Recents and Favorites tabs. The compounds you look up twice shouldn't cost two lookups.

Every identifier, one tap to copy

  • Name, formula, MW, CID, and CAS at the top — not buried three cards down.
  • SMILES (full and connectivity), InChI, InChIKey, empirical formula, formal charge — one section further. All tap-to-copy.
  • Tooltip on each field explains what it means, so you don't need to alt-tab to a wiki page.

3D that actually behaves

  • 2D structure loads instantly. Tap to zoom in. Switch to 3D when you want to look at the actual geometry.
  • Drag to rotate, pinch to zoom. Auto-spin with pause on touch. It doesn't turn into soup when you try to inspect a bond angle.
  • CPK colors for all 118 elements. Download the SDF or PNG directly to your device.

GHS safety data that doesn't need a manual

  • Signal word badge (Danger / Warning), GHS pictograms 1–9, and H-codes — all fetched from PubChem directly, no paraphrasing middleware.
  • Useful when you need the hazard summary before you reach the MSDS, not after.

Stays out of your way

  • Light or dark theme. Configurable default description source. Settings that actually remember what you set.
  • Local compound cache speeds up repeat lookups and labels cached results in the UI so you know what you're seeing.

Optional AI — not a subscription trap

  • Works completely offline from AI — PubChem and Wikipedia descriptions are there by default.
  • Add your own Gemini or Groq key in Settings if you want AI-generated summaries with a regenerate button. Your key stays on your device.
  • No fake "smart" layer bolted over the data. No key farming.

11 tools built in

The chemistry drawer

The calculations you'd usually hunt down a separate app (or a pencil) for. Most of the standard classroom and lab math is already here, in the Tools tab.

Tip: press / or Ctrl/Cmd + K to jump to this filter.

No tools matched that filter.

Custom 3D Molecule Viewer
Load any .mol or .sdf file from your device and explore it in the same native 3D engine used for PubChem compounds.
Molar Mass Calculator
Handles parentheses, hydrate dot notation, and the formulas people write when focus has well and truly left the building.
Oxidation State Finder
Covers peroxides, superoxides, ozonides, metal hydrides, and interhalogen compounds. Accepts overall charge for polyatomic ions.
SMILES Visualizer
Paste a SMILES string, get the 2D structure and 3D model from PubChem. Useful when you have the notation but not the name.
Reaction Balancer
Gaussian elimination with exact rational arithmetic. Quick-insert buttons for common symbols; swap-sides control included.
Isomer Finder
Enter a molecular formula and get up to 20 structural isomers back. Also accessible by tapping the formula on any compound page.
Limiting Reagent
Identifies which reagent runs out first, the molar ratios, and the theoretical yield — for a balanced equation you provide.
Percent Yield
Quick reality check between theoretical and actual yield. For when the bench disagrees with the calculation.
Reaction Scaling
Scale reactant amounts for a target product quantity, without rewriting every number by hand.
Dilution Calculator
C₁V₁ = C₂V₂, solved for any variable. For when the formula evaporates the moment you need it most.
Ideal Gas Law
PV = nRT, solves for any one unknown. Covers the standard unit conversions so you don't have to.

PubChem PUG REST Official PubChem endpoint docs. Compound lookup, properties, autocomplete, synonyms, SDF and structure files.

Wikipedia REST API Fallback for plain-language summaries. Compound descriptions when PubChem's entries are sparse.
Gemini API Optional — also supports Groq Bring-your-own-key AI summaries. The app runs fully without these.
GitHub Releases API Release metadata endpoint. Latest APK version check and update status shown in the header.

Requirements

  • Android 8.0+ (API 26+), internet connection
  • Android Studio Hedgehog+ to build from source
  • No API keys required to use the app

Permissions

  • INTERNET — compound data and release checks
  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS — optional update alerts
  • WRITE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE — Android 9 and below only

Privacy

No analytics, no telemetry, no creepy extras. Favorites, search history, cache, and API keys live on your device. Data goes directly to PubChem, Wikipedia, Gemini, or Groq — no intermediate servers.
