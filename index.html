The useful bits

Not a chemistry encyclopedia pretending to be an app. Just the things you actually reach for when you need an answer quickly.

Search without guesswork

  • Look up compounds by common name, IUPAC name, CAS, or CID.
  • Autocomplete helps when the spelling gets ugly.
  • Favorites and recent searches keep repeat lookups quick.

IDs where you need them

  • Formula, molar mass, CID, and CAS show up first, not buried.
  • SMILES, InChI, InChIKey, charge, and empirical formula are one tap away.
  • Tap-to-copy is everywhere it ought to be.

3D that behaves itself

  • View the 2D structure fast, then jump into the 3D model.
  • Rotate, pinch, and inspect without the whole thing turning into soup.
  • All 118 elements use proper CPK colors, so it looks like chemistry and not confetti.

Safety notes you can actually read

  • GHS signal words, pictograms, and hazard codes are laid out cleanly.
  • Descriptions can come from PubChem, Wikipedia, or your own AI provider.
  • Useful when you need the summary first and the paper later.

Keeps your place

  • Light or dark theme, whichever hurts your eyes less.
  • Pick a default description source and leave it there.
  • Cached results make repeat lookups less annoying.

Optional AI, not a hostage situation

  • The app works fine without AI turned on.
  • If you do want extra summaries, add your own Gemini or Groq key.
  • No key farming, no fake "smart" layer glued over everything.

What you need

  • Android 8.0+ (API 26+)
  • An internet connection for lookups and descriptions
  • Android Studio Hedgehog or newer if you want to build it yourself

Permissions

  • INTERNET for compound data and release checks
  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS for optional update alerts
  • WRITE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE on Android 9 and below

Privacy

No analytics, no telemetry, no creepy extras. Favorites, search history, cache, and provider keys stay on the device unless you deliberately use an external API.
