# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

ChemSearch's website is for Android users who want a focused chemistry reference app: students, teachers, hobbyists, and lab-prep users who need compound lookup, structures, safety notes, saved records, and quick calculations on a phone.

## Product Purpose

The site should explain what ChemSearch does, show the real Android interface, and get visitors to the latest APK or the GitHub repository quickly. Success means a regular user understands the app without build jargon, trusts the screenshots, and can download the app without hunting.

## Brand Personality

Clear, technical, and practical. The page should feel like a careful chemistry tool, not a generic SaaS landing page. It should keep ChemSearch's dark blue Material-inspired identity while staying readable on small screens.

## Anti-references

Avoid AI-looking landing page habits: repeated tiny uppercase section labels, excessive card grids, generic marketing copy, oversized glow effects, decorative motion, and technical details regular users do not need. Do not hide restored sections on mobile. Do not drift away from the existing blue ChemSearch theme.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the app itself: real screenshots, direct download, and plain Android support wording.
2. Keep the page calm enough for low-end devices: use small purposeful transitions, not jumping or glow-heavy effects.
3. Make mobile the primary reading path: no horizontal scroll, large touch targets, readable type, and all sections available.
4. Use chemistry-specific substance over generic claims: mention lookup, structures, safety data, offline saves, reference data, and tools.
5. Preserve trust: direct GitHub links, clear source notes, no vague promises.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target practical WCAG AA behavior: strong contrast in dark and light themes, visible focus states, keyboard navigation, reduced-motion support, alt text for screenshots, and touch targets at least 44px tall.
